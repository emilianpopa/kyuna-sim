How the model works. Both scenarios assume the same monthly cash capacity. In BUY, that capacity goes to mortgage + maintenance. In RENT + INVEST, the rent is paid and the surplus (BUY cost minus rent) is invested at the equity return rate. The initial cash that would have been used as down payment + fees is invested instead in the RENT scenario. After the mortgage tenor ends, BUY also invests the freed cash flow (former mortgage equivalent minus maintenance). House value in USD reflects KES depreciation against USD. "Net wealth" is asset value minus outstanding debt minus prior cash sunk that didn't become equity.
